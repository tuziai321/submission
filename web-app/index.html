Game Rules

1. Place Pieces

Red and Blue begin with two fixed pieces each. The remaining pieces are placed in this order: Red, Blue, Blue, Red.

2. Move

On your turn, choose one of your pieces and move it zero, one, or two squares in a straight orthogonal line.

3. Build a Wall

After moving, build one wall on an open edge beside the piece you moved. Walls are permanent, block future movement, and are coloured by the player who built them.

4. Win Territory

The game ends when each connected region contains pieces from at most one player, when a lead cannot be caught, or when the current player has no legal moves. Single-player regions score territory for that player.

5. Scoring

Walls split the board into connected territories. Each cell in a territory is worth 1 point, but it only scores if that territory contains pieces from one player only.

Scoring Example

If Red walls off a 6-cell area with only Red pieces inside, Red scores 6 points. If a 5-cell area contains both Red and Blue, neither player scores those 5 cells.
